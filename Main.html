Choosing the Right Windlass

When selecting a windlass there are a number of factors that need to be considered: vessel type, length and displacement, anchor and chain size, windage and the anchoring environment.

Choose Your Windlass Power: Manual or Electric?

When it comes to powering your windlass, you have three main options.
1. Manual Windlasses: These above deck units require some physical effort, but internal gearing or a ratchet drive system can make it easier. They are simple to install.
2. Electric Power Windlasses: Powered by motors that do most of the work, these windlasses can be controlled with remote or deck switches, minimizing user effort. Electric models require specific electrical power sources.
3. Hydraulic Power Windlasses: Utilizing a hydraulic pump or power pack, these windlasses provide another powered option for anchoring.
